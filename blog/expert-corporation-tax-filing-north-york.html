FAQs

What taxes must North York corporations file?

Corporations in North York must file a T2 Corporate Income Tax Return with the CRA every year, even if there is no income or activity. Depending on the business, additional filings may include HST returns, payroll remittances, T4/T5 slips, and provincial corporate tax schedules. Missing or incorrect filings can trigger penalties or CRA follow-ups.

When are corporate tax filings due?

The T2 return is due six months after the corporation’s fiscal year-end, but any corporate taxes owing must be paid within two or three months, depending on eligibility. Filing late—even with no balance owing—can result in penalties. Timely filing is critical for maintaining good CRA standing and avoiding unnecessary interest.

How can expert corporate tax filing reduce audit risk?

Professional corporate tax filing helps reduce audits by ensuring accurate financial reporting, proper expense categorization, and CRA-compliant schedules. Experienced Accounting Firm also apply consistent accounting methods year over year, flag potential red areas in advance, and maintain organized documentation—making your business less likely to attract CRA scrutiny.

Do corporations need to file a T2 if there is no income?

Yes. All incorporated businesses in Canada, including those in North York, must file a T2 return every year, even if the company had no income, expenses, or activity. Failure to file can result in penalties and compliance issues.

What happens if a corporation files late?

Late corporate tax filings can result in penalties, interest charges, and increased CRA attention. Repeated late filings may also impact the corporation’s ability to claim certain deductions or credits in the future.

Are HST filings part of corporate tax filing?

HST filings are separate from the T2 return but are often prepared alongside corporate taxes. Many North York corporations choose to have both handled together to ensure consistency between sales, expenses, and reported tax amounts.

Can a Accounting Firm help with past-due or missed corporate filings?

Yes. A Accounting Firm can help catch up on overdue T2 returns, correct previously filed errors, negotiate with the CRA when appropriate, and bring the corporation back into compliance while minimizing penalties.

Do small corporations need professional tax filing support?

Even small or owner-managed corporations benefit from professional filing. Accounting Firm help identify legitimate tax deductions, ensure compliance, and prevent costly mistakes that often occur with DIY or software-only filings.

What documents are needed for corporate tax filing?

Common documents include financial statements, bank records, expense receipts, payroll summaries, HST reports, prior-year tax returns, and shareholder information. A Accounting Firm helps organize and review these to ensure accurate reporting.

10. How often should corporations review their tax strategy?

Ideally, corporations should review their tax strategy at least once a year, and more often if the business is growing, changing structure, or making large financial decisions. Ongoing planning helps reduce taxes legally and avoid surprises at year-end.

Understanding North York Corporate Tax Filing: Deadlines and Requirements

If you run a corporation in North York, you need to follow certain tax deadlines from the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA). Filing your annual corporate tax return, called the T2 return, is a must for any active business in Ontario. Filing on time keeps you clear of penalties and helps your company stay in good standing.

Here are some important dates and tasks:

  • Corporate Tax Return Due Date: Most corporations must file their T2 returns within six months after their fiscal year ends.
  • Tax Payment Deadline: Taxes usually must be paid two months after year-end. But small CCPCs get three months to pay.
  • T2 Schedules Preparation: These are extra forms that come with your T2 return. They show details about income, deductions, credits, and other info.

If you miss these deadlines, you can get hit with penalties. They start at 5% of the unpaid tax plus 1% more each month. Interest will also add up on what you owe.

What’s involved in annual corporate tax filings?

Every North York company needs to put together a complete T2 package every year. This includes:

  • Filling out all required schedules based on what your business does
  • Reporting taxable income correctly
  • Claiming any tax credits your company can use
  • Making sure you submit electronically as CRA wants

Hiring a Accounting Firm helps make sure everything fits the rules and that you don’t miss chances to save on taxes.
